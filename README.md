# SQL-Murder-Mystery-Project
In this project ,I took on the role of a lead data analyst investigating the mysterious death of the CEO of TechNova Inc.. The objective is to utilise SQL to analyse multiple datasets — including keycard logs, phone records, alibis, and evidence — to determine who committed the crime, where and when it occurred, and how it happened.

## 🎬 Case Overview  
On the night of **October 15, 2025**, the CEO of **Tech Nova Inc.** was found dead inside his office at exactly **9:00 PM**.  

No eyewitnesses. No obvious suspects.  
Only digital evidence scattered across internal systems.  

This project simulates a real-world investigative scenario where SQL is used to reconstruct events, validate claims, and uncover the truth.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective  
Leverage SQL and analytical thinking to:
- Reconstruct the timeline of events  
- Identify suspicious employee activity  
- Validate alibis using data  
- Analyze communication patterns  
- Determine the perpetrator  

---

## 🗂️ Data Sources  
The investigation relied on the following tables:

- 👤 `employees` — Employee details  
- 🔑 `keycard_logs` — Room access records  
- 📞 `calls` — Communication logs  
- 🧾 `alibis` — Claimed locations of employees  
- 🔍 `evidence` — Crime scene findings  
 

---
## 🛠️ Key SQL Concepts Used  
- `SELECT`, `WHERE`, `ORDER BY`, `LIMIT`  
- `INNER JOIN` (multi-table joins)  
- Subqueries  
- `LIKE` and pattern matching  
- Aggregations and filtering  
- Logical condition building


---

## 🔍 Investigation Approach  

### 1️⃣ Crime Scene Analysis  
Established the **time and location** of the incident to define the investigation window.

---

### 2️⃣ Access Log Investigation  
Analyzed entry records to identify individuals present near the CEO’s office during the critical timeframe.  

📌 *Result:* Narrowed down to a highly specific suspect group.

---

### 3️⃣ Alibi Validation  
Cross-referenced claimed locations with actual movement data.  

📌 *Result:* Identified inconsistencies between reported and actual behavior.

---

### 4️⃣ Communication Analysis  
Reviewed call logs to detect unusual or suspicious communication patterns before the incident.  

📌 *Result:* Discovered high-risk interactions shortly before the crime.

---

### 5️⃣ Evidence Correlation  
Aligned physical evidence with employee movement and behavioral data.  

📌 *Result:* Confirmed contradictions that pointed to a single suspect.

---

## 🧠 Final Outcome  

### 🕵️ Killer Identified: **David Kumar**

**Key Evidence:**
- ❌ False alibi (claimed vs actual location mismatch)  
- 🔑 Presence in CEO office during critical timeframe  
- 📞 Suspicious calls leading up to the incident  
- 📊 Data inconsistencies across multiple sources
